A Brief History of Open Source

1967 - IBM Airline Control Program

  • Hardware and software tightly coupled
  • Open Source by necessity
  • Academic influence of openness and collaboration

1976 - "Open Letter to Hobbyists"

  • Hardware becoming more standardized
  • "Software business" is A Thing
  • Computers beginning to spread outside of academia and large corporations

1985 - Free Software Foundation

  • Designed to protect user freedom
  • Retain academic spirit of openness and sharing
  • "Social movement"; not very business friendly

1998 - Netscape Open Sources their Browser

  • Hardware becoming more standardized
  • "Software business" is A Thing
  • Computers beginning to spread outside of academia and large corporations

2014 - Microsoft Open Sources .NET Core

  • Almost the total opposite of "Open Letter to Hobbyists"
  • Open Source enjoys large corporate backing and support
  • Business friendly licenses prevail
  • Businesses creating value using software, but not selling software directly

#1 Advice - Social Skills and Communication

  • Pervasive problem in our industry
  • Has a direct impact on the software you write
  • Make your team better
  • Learn to negotiate
  • Get outside of your comfort zone
"Their [software developers] principal work is human communication to organize the user's expressions of needs into formal procedure."
Peopleware, 1987

Work on "The Right Thing"

"Working on the wrong thing for a month is equivalent to not showing up to work for a month at all."
Slava Akhmechet

Specialize and Generalize

Work at a Software Company

Work somewhere where the quality of the software effects the bottom line.

Working software gets you hired

  • Open source
  • Working website
  • Side projects
  • It's a smell if you don't have any of these

Tech "pop culture" considered harmful

  • Read books, not blogs
  • Focus on "timeless advice"
  • Nobody becomes an expert on distributed systems by reading Hacker News or Twitter

Education never stops

  • It's up to you
  • Knowledge is compounding; don't undervalue it
  • Increase your ability to create value
  • Learn by reading, but mostly doing
  • Get outside of your comfort zone
